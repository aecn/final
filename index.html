TARGETED ADS: EXPLAINED

are you a victim?

Consider this scenario: You want to buy a new pair of fashionable sneakers, so you search up different styles on Google. During this search, you browse various store websites, fashionable social media accounts, and any pages related to sneakers. You decide to either purchase a pair or just leave it be - maybe you'll look again tomorrow. However, in the next hours to the next few days, you start to see ads for fashionable sneakers pop up everywhere - on your Facebook, Instagram, and every website that runs ads. No matter what you do on the web, these ads seem to follow you everywhere, and it changes according to what you've been interested in lately. What exactly is going on? If this scenario is familiar to you, then you are a victim of behavioral targeting!

BEHAVIORAL TARGETING

What exactly is behavioral targeting? Behavioral targeting is an online advertising and publishing technique where data from visitor browsing habits is used to display relevant ads and offers. Visitor browsing habits include search terms, previous websites visited, and previous purchases. The goal of behavioral targeting is to improve advertising effectiveness. However, this technique has a lot of problems, which will be explained in the later section.

BEHAVIORAL RETARGETING

Now we move on to a concept called behavioral retargeting. Behavioral retargeting uses the same data from behavioral targeting to re-engage visitors that have already shown interest in something. Behavioral retargeting targets online ads to consumers based on their previous internet behavior. There is a pattern to all of your browsing habits - and this pattern of behavior is being used to further personalize the ads that you see.

HOW IS THIS POSSIBLE?

WHY THIS IS TERRIBLE

  • Many people have expressed fear and loathing of this technique's creepy data collection practices
  • People lose their online privacy; there's no choice, control, or ownership left
  • Personal information is being exploited for the sake of commercial purposes
  • It's virtually impossible for people to opt out of being tracked due to zombie cookies
  • There's a lack of notice: people do not get explicit warnings or disclaimers about their activities being tracked
  • Even for companies, it's not very advantageous. It's very expensive to target niche audiences

HOW TO PREVENT TARGETED ADS

• Download ad blocking extensions: Adblock or uBlock Origin

• Periodically clear your cookies on your browsers and devices

• Don't allow cookies

• Delete your search history

• Opt out of personalized ads

• Use an anonymous VPN

BE MINDFUL OF BEHAVIORAL TARGETING!
